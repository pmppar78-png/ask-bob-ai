About Ask Bob AI

Ask Bob AI is a free AI assistant built around a simple frustration — the one most homeowners hit the first time they try to plan a real project. You Google "how to replace a bathroom faucet" and get back a 40-second clip that skips the shutoff valve step, a forum thread arguing about Teflon tape direction, and an ad for a plumber. None of it answers the actual question: given my 1994 copper supply lines and my total lack of soldering experience, can I do this safely in an afternoon? Bob is the assistant that engages with that kind of specific situation. You describe your project, your space, your skill level, and your tools, and Bob works through it with you — calling out the permit gates, the code minimums, the tool you probably don't own yet, and the point at which the honest answer becomes "call a licensed pro." It's not a replacement for professional judgment. It's the friend-who-works-in-the-trades that most people don't have on speed dial.

Our Mission

Make planning a home or auto project less intimidating. That's it. We think most homeowners want to do more work themselves and would, if they had a clearer picture of what's actually involved — costs, sequence, codes, and the realistic point where a DIY job becomes a pro call. Bob's job is to give them that picture, without the upsell and without the confidence gap. Read our take on when to DIY and when to hire out for the decision framework Bob uses.

Why Ask Bob AI Exists

The internet has endless home-improvement content, but most of it is tuned for watch-time, not for getting a real project finished. A 12-minute YouTube video on rewiring a three-way switch shows you the after, not the hour of disconnecting the wrong neutrals first. A how-to blog skips the permit question because it would break the "easy DIY" framing. Scattered content assumes a baseline you may not have yet.

Bob is conversational for a reason. When you plan a deck build or troubleshoot a failing HVAC system, the right answer depends on your house, your climate, your code jurisdiction, and your experience — not on whatever generic steps rank on Google this week. Bob asks the follow-ups that a real tradesperson would ask, then builds the plan around the answers. For the reference-level material people read before they open a conversation, we publish step-by-step guides — linked from every page.

What Makes Bob Different From a Generic Chatbot

Two things. First, Bob is tuned specifically for home and auto work — the prompts, safety guardrails, and code references are written for this domain rather than applied as general-purpose reasoning. A general chatbot will cheerfully explain how to replace a circuit breaker; Bob will ask what panel brand you have first (because the Federal Pacific Stab-Lok and Zinsco recalls are not an edge case), and will redirect you to an electrician if the honest answer is that the work should be permitted.

Second, Bob is conservative on safety. You will not get a workaround for a missing jack stand. You will not get shortcuts on electrical work that requires permits. That's deliberate — we'd rather send a user to a licensed pro than get the click on a dangerous DIY. We consider it a product failure when Bob gives confident advice that a real contractor would push back on.

How We Work

Safety is the ceiling

If a project has a non-trivial injury, fire, or structural-failure risk, Bob defaults to recommending a licensed pro — even when it shortens a conversation. Saving $400 is not worth a house fire.

Honest about limits

Bob is a planning and learning tool. It is not a licensed contractor, not a mechanic, and not a substitute for in-person professional assessment. We say that clearly in the disclaimer and Bob will say it mid-conversation when relevant.

Teach the "why"

Knowing that drain plugs torque to 20-30 ft-lbs is useful; understanding that over-torquing strips the pan and turns a $40 job into a $600 job is why you'll remember it next year.

Free, honestly

No paywall, no login required for chat. We may run display ads and may include affiliate links in guide content; those relationships never change the guidance Bob gives you.

Who Builds and Maintains Ask Bob AI

Ask Bob AI is an independent publisher project based in Boston, Massachusetts. We're not a VC-backed startup, we don't have a media parent company, and we don't operate a network of sites. Our guide content is reviewed against published code references (IRC, NEC, IPC, OSHA) and manufacturer specifications before it goes live, with corrections welcomed at the contact page. Our editorial approach — sourcing, corrections, AI transparency — is documented on the editorial standards page.

Bob itself is built on a large language model tuned with a prompt system focused on home and auto work, with explicit instructions to defer to licensed professionals when a question crosses into life-safety, code-permit, or structural territory. The AI generates its responses in real time; it does not index your personal data or retain your conversations for training.

How We Sustain the Service

Ask Bob AI is free to use. We may display ads from advertising partners (clearly separated from editorial content), and some guide pages may include affiliate links to tools or materials — if you buy through those links, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. Affiliate relationships never change which tools Bob recommends or which materials a guide covers. You will also see clear product names in our guides (Purdy, Klein, DeWalt, Mobil 1, etc.); we include those because they are the brands contractors actually specify, not because they sponsor us.

Questions, Corrections, or Partnerships

We want to hear where the site is wrong, where Bob's advice was less than helpful, and where a guide missed a detail that mattered. Reach us via the contact page — we read every message and publish corrections promptly when needed.
